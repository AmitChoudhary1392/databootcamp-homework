Humidity

Across latitudes, there are always places where relative humidity is 100%, which means that the air is fully saturated with water and is very likely to rain or snow. Up to 5-10 degrees latitude on both sides of the equator, the weather is consistently humid, because high temperature, abundant waterbodies, and dense forest bring lots of water vapor to the air. Further from the equator, there is a wide range of humidity in both northern and southern hemisphere.
